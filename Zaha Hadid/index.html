ZAHA HADID

TRIBUTE

In memory of one of the greatest modern architects of our time.

Zaha Hadid was a visionary architect known for her groundbreaking designs and innovative approach to architecture. This is a tribute to Hadid and the contributions she has made to the world.

BIO

1950

Born in Iraq in 1950, Hadid's distinctive style was characterized by fluid lines, bold curves, and futuristic forms. She pushed the boundaries of traditional architecture, creating structures that appeared to defy gravity and challenge conventional notions of space.

1972

Hadid began her studies at the Architectural Association School of Architecture in London, United Kingdom in 1972 and graduated with a Diploma in Architecture in 1977. Her studies provided a strong foundation for her later career as a groundbreaking architect and helped shape her unique vision and approach to design.

1993

Zaha Hadid designed her first building, the Vitra Fire Station in Weil am Rhein, Germany, in 1993. The completion of this project marked a significant milestone in her career as it was her first major built work.

2004

Throughout her career, she received numerous prestigious awards, including the Pritzker Architecture Prize in 2004, making her the first woman to ever receive the honor.

2016

Hadid passed away in 2016, but her visionary spirit lives on through her iconic structures and the countless architects she has influenced.
